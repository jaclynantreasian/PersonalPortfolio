Problem

A local meteorologist has a great fanbase on social media. He starts getting a lot of messages how people are frustrated with their weather apps, they are riddled with ads and lack a focus on local weather.

Solution

A meteorologist in your pocket, personalized and hyper local weather crafted by a local meteorologist. NSWS focuses on local weather and includes current, daily and hourly forecasts a detailed storm center with models and analysis, live ligthning and so much more!

FIRST IMPRESSIONS

Great idea, huge usability issues

When my client first invited me on board with his project, he told me was nearly complete and only needed a logo and perhaps some icons to finish. Once I installed and troubleshot the app, I found a plethora of major usability issues. The usability issues combined with my client's need to launch this app as soon as possible due to budget issues, made this project a real test in problem solving and unveiling the biggest UX problems to fix. When I first opened the page on the website I encountered an unauthorized page and knew I had lots of work to do

After a complete assessment I uncovered numerous usability issues, with these three being the most major.

  • The current conditions map defaults to the closest weather statiion but doesn't show the user's location, this can confuse users.
  • the home screen has a width over 100% and the user has the ability to scroll left and right.
  • The menu for app navigation is incredibly long and can condensed to provide optimal usability when switching between numerous weather forecasts.
  • weather maps are also very difficult to navigate, with a higher maximum width and not defaulting to the user's current area.

DISCOVERY

User survey

My client felt he had a good idea of what his users were looking for, after years of getting requests for an app and app features. I recommended we still do a survey and put it out to his 8K followers to get a better idea of where their pain points were, and what features would be great to include in the new app.

Love detailed weather reports

Use apps to get their weather

Wish that weather coverage was more local.

Out of the 64 responses we got, I noticed that a lot of people use weather apps for their main way to get the weather, but were frustrated with a clunky design filled with information they didn't need yet the apps didn't have the local coverage they are looking for. Users loved the idea of having in depth storm analysis and live lightning, and we decided in depth storm coverage would be an important feature of the app.

Considering the responses to the survey, I needed a little more information on our competitors. I did an indepth competitive analysis of The Weather Channel App, Accuweather app and a local news app. What I found was lots of ads and cluttered content that didn't focus to on local weater (especially with the Weather Channel app, which the majority of respondants use), text that was incredibly difficult to read (WCVB) and with accuweather hard to understand and interpret forecasts. Users needed a legible, relevant and easy to understand app with lots of relevant content with a focus on understanding the science behind big storms and an emphasis on local weather. After the competitive analysis my client and I were confident we could create an effective app for his uers.

User Personas

To get an idea of the needs of different users, I developed some user personas.

Bud, 68, Retired

"Don't like the weather? Just wait a minute"

Bud considers himself a North Shore staple, He was here for the blizzard of 78, and all the major hurricanes Nor’Easters and “scorchah summahs”. He understands that weather can make an impact on daily life, and likes to be prepared when taking care of his grandchildren and fishing on his boat in the summer. Bud isn’t a big fan of technology and prefers to be working with his hands on real-life projects rather than scrolling through a smartphone and while he as used weather apps in the past he feels they too general and wrong way too often. He needs an app that is suepr easy to use, reliable and from a forecaster with a good local reputation..

    Goals
  • Wants a reliable forecast
  • Wants to know where he is getting his weather from.
  • Wants a weatherman who is born and raised watching the same skies as him is a plus.
      • Frustrations
  • Feels that weather apps lack quality checks.
  • Doesn’t usually enjoy using a smartphone and feels apps are usually not worth the hassle
  • Feels TV meteorologists are more celebrities than forecasters these days
    • Bill, 38, EMT dispatcher

    "Knowing the weather can help me keep people safe"

    An EMT dispatcher by day, combined with an active lifestyle keep Bill busy. Bill doesn't have time to watch meteorologists on TV and relies on weather apps to get the information he needs. He uses the Weather Channel App, and enjoys the push notifications so that he knows how to prepare but find it doesn't provides enough updates on what to expect, especially during storms. Being a dispatcher makes understanding road conditions to be very important and he wishes there was a way to get the scoop with just the click of a button.

      Goals
  • Wishes he could see road conditions, hourly accumulation and other essential information for workers that need to drive in all weather quickly and safely.
  • Wants weather predictions at least 24 hours in advance
  • Wants a quick way to access important developments
      • Frustrations
  • Feels that weather apps are too general to give detailed information on local conditions.
  • Always scrolling through information that isn't relevant
    • Evelyn, 43, Chemistry Teacher

    "Got to get the bread and milk!"

    Evelyn is a Chemistry Teacher in her home town of Marblehead, where she grew up and recently decided to buy her first home at. She loves living by the sea and spends most of her time at the beach no matter the weather or season when she isn’t working. Evelyn loves getting as much information as she can about the weather, and will watch the weather channel and surf social media for the latest updates on the biggest storms. Evelyn occasionally uses the weather apps, but prefers to watch the meteorologists on TV because they have a lot more information on local weather. She would love a one stop shop where she can follow the storm on the go and share her awesome ocean storm shots with fellow weather geeks.

      Goals
  • Wants to see local weather on the go and in real time
  • Wants live storm coverage
  • Sharing ocean storm shots with fellow weather geeks.
      • Frustrations
  • Hates feeling like she needs to be glued to the TV to get detailed local weather.
  • Loves the format and trustworthiness of meteorologists on social media but is trying to use Facebook less often
